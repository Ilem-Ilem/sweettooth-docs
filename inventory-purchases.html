Creating Purchase Orders

Process Overview

Creating purchase orders involves specifying supplier information, items to be purchased, and associated costs.

Required Information:

  • Purchase Date: Date of the purchase order
  • Supplier Name: Name of the supplier
  • Supplier Contact: Contact information for the supplier
  • Items: List of items to be purchased with quantities and prices
  • Other Costs: Additional costs like shipping, taxes, etc.
  • Payment Status: Expected payment status (paid, partial, pending)

Steps to Create a Purchase Order:

  1. Navigate to the Purchases section
  2. Click the "Add New Purchase" button
  3. Fill in the supplier information
  4. Add items to the purchase order using the "Add Item" button
  5. Specify quantity, unit of measure, and unit price for each item
  6. Enter any additional costs in the "Other Costs" field
  7. Set the expected payment status
  8. Click "Save" to create the purchase order
  9. If you're a non-super admin, submit for approval

Item Selection:

  • Select items from the existing inventory items list
  • Quantities must be positive numbers
  • Unit prices must be positive numbers
  • Unit of measure will auto-populate based on the selected item

Tip: The system automatically calculates the purchase number based on your branch code and current date. This ensures unique identification for each purchase order.

Important: Non-super admin users will save purchases as "draft" status and need to request approval. Super admins can create purchases directly with "approved" status.

Purchase Workflow

Process Overview

The purchase workflow varies depending on your user role and the approval requirements in place.

Super Admin Workflow:

  1. Create purchase order with items and supplier details
  2. Save directly with "approved" status
  3. Stock levels update automatically upon creation
  4. Transaction is complete

Regular User Workflow:

  1. Create purchase order with items and supplier details
  2. Save as "draft" status
  3. Click "Request Approval" to submit for review
  4. Wait for approval from authorized personnel
  5. Upon approval, stock levels update automatically

Approval Process:

  • Purchase orders in "draft" status require approval
  • Authorized personnel review the purchase details
  • Approvals are processed through the Audit Management section
  • Upon approval, stock levels update automatically
  • Financial records are updated accordingly

Best Practice: Always verify supplier details and item information before submitting for approval to avoid delays in the process.

Managing Purchase Orders

Process Overview

Once purchase orders are created, you can view details, track status, and manage related activities.

Viewing Purchase Details:

  • Click on a purchase order in the list to view details
  • See all items included in the order
  • View supplier information and contact details
  • Check the status of the purchase order
  • Review cost breakdown and payment status

Managing Draft Purchases:

  • Draft purchases can be edited before approval
  • Draft purchases can be deleted if no longer needed
  • Only draft purchases can be deleted
  • Once approved, purchases become immutable

Actions Available:

  • View Details: See complete purchase information
  • Edit (Draft Only): Modify purchase details before approval
  • Delete (Draft Only): Remove draft purchases
  • Request Approval: Submit draft for review
  • Export: Download purchase data in Excel or CSV

Important: Once a purchase order is approved, it cannot be edited or deleted. Only draft purchases can be modified.

Purchase Approvals

Process Overview

Purchase approvals ensure proper authorization for procurement activities and financial controls.

Approval Process:

  1. User creates purchase order and saves as draft
  2. User clicks "Request Approval" for the specific purchase
  3. Approval request is sent to authorized personnel
  4. Authorized personnel review the request in Audit Management
  5. Approve or reject the purchase request
  6. Upon approval, stock levels update automatically

Approval Requirements:

  • All non-super admin users must get approval for purchases
  • Purchases in "draft" status require approval
  • Approval requests include purchase details and reason
  • Approvals are tracked in the audit log

What Happens After Approval:

  • Purchase status changes to "approved"
  • Stock levels update automatically based on received quantities
  • Financial records update with purchase costs
  • Stock movement records are created
  • Inventory valuation adjusts based on new stock

Tip: Provide detailed reasons when requesting approval to help approvers make informed decisions quickly.

Payment Status Tracking

Overview

Track the payment status of purchase orders to manage cash flow and supplier relationships.

Payment Status Options:

  • Paid: Full payment has been made to the supplier
  • Partial: Partial payment has been made
  • Pending: Payment is due but not yet made

Setting Payment Status:

  • Set payment status when creating the purchase order
  • Update payment status as payments are made
  • Payment status is visible in purchase lists and reports
  • Payment status helps with accounts payable tracking

Payment Tracking Benefits:

  • Monitor outstanding payments to suppliers
  • Track cash flow related to inventory purchases
  • Identify overdue payments that need attention
  • Improve supplier relationship management

Best Practice: Regularly update payment statuses to maintain accurate financial records and avoid late payment penalties.

Filters and Search

Overview

The Purchases interface includes filtering and search capabilities to help you find specific orders quickly.

Available Filters:

  • Search: Search by purchase number or supplier name
  • Payment Status Filter: Filter by payment status (paid, partial, pending)
  • Status Filter: Filter by purchase status (draft, approved, etc.)

Using Filters:

  1. Use the search bar to find purchases by number or supplier
  2. Select a payment status from the dropdown to filter
  3. Select a purchase status to filter by lifecycle stage
  4. Combine multiple filters for more specific results

Additional Features:

  • Sort purchases by clicking on column headers
  • Adjust the number of purchases displayed per page
  • Export filtered results to Excel or CSV
  • View purchase summary statistics

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Purchases features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to create, approve, and manage purchases directly
  • Inventory Manager: Can create purchases but requires approval for most actions
  • Procurement Officer: Dedicated role for purchase management
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Higher-level roles can approve purchases from lower-level users
  • Financial approval may be required for purchases above certain thresholds
  • Supplier management permissions may be separate from purchase permissions

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for purchase management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Purchase Management

Follow these best practices to maintain efficient and effective purchase management.

Planning Purchases:

  • Align purchases with inventory needs and reorder levels
  • Consider bulk purchasing discounts for frequently used items
  • Factor in lead times when planning purchases
  • Coordinate with budget cycles and financial planning

Supplier Management:

  • Maintain accurate supplier contact information
  • Track supplier performance and reliability
  • Negotiate payment terms that align with cash flow
  • Keep backup suppliers for critical items

Cost Management:

  • Include all costs (shipping, taxes, duties) in landed cost calculation
  • Compare prices from multiple suppliers when possible
  • Take advantage of early payment discounts
  • Monitor cost trends for budget planning

Documentation:

  • Provide clear reasons when requesting purchase approval
  • Keep purchase records organized for auditing
  • Document any special terms or conditions with suppliers

Pro Tip: Regularly review purchase history to identify spending patterns and optimize procurement strategies.
