General Ledger

Overview

The General Ledger report provides a detailed view of all account transactions and balances.

Key Features:

  • Show all transactions for selected accounts
  • Display running balances for each account
  • Filter by date range, account, or transaction type
  • Group transactions by category or department
  • Include opening balances in the report
  • Export to Excel for further analysis

Report Options:

  • Date Range: Specify the period for the report
  • Account Selection: Choose specific accounts or all accounts
  • Detail Level: Show summary or detailed transactions
  • Opening Balance: Include or exclude opening balances
  • Sorting: Sort by date, account, or transaction amount

Tip: Use the General Ledger to investigate specific account balances and verify transaction accuracy.

Trial Balance

Overview

The Trial Balance report verifies that debits equal credits in the general ledger.

Key Features:

  • Show all account balances at a specific point in time
  • Verify that total debits equal total credits
  • Identify potential errors in posting
  • Filter by date range or account type
  • Include zero-balance accounts
  • Export for audit purposes

Report Options:

  • As of Date: Select the date for the trial balance
  • Account Type: Filter by asset, liability, equity, revenue, or expense
  • Zero Balances: Include or exclude zero-balance accounts
  • Comparison: Compare with prior period balances

Tip: Run the Trial Balance regularly to ensure your books are balanced before preparing financial statements.

Income Statement

Overview

The Income Statement (Profit & Loss) shows revenues, expenses, and net income over a period.

Key Features:

  • Display revenues and expenses for the selected period
  • Calculate gross profit, operating income, and net income
  • Compare with prior period performance
  • Break down by department or cost center
  • Include budget versus actual comparisons
  • Export for presentations or analysis

Report Options:

  • Period: Monthly, quarterly, or annual reporting
  • Comparison: Compare with prior period or budget
  • Detail Level: Summary or detailed presentation
  • Department: Break down by organizational unit

Tip: Use the Income Statement to monitor profitability trends and identify areas for improvement.

Balance Sheet

Overview

The Balance Sheet shows assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.

Key Features:

  • Display assets, liabilities, and equity in standard format
  • Verify that assets equal liabilities plus equity
  • Compare with prior period balances
  • Include comparative columns for trend analysis
  • Break down by detail level as needed
  • Export for stakeholder reporting

Report Options:

  • Date: Select the date for the balance sheet
  • Format: Standard or comparative presentation
  • Detail Level: Summary or detailed breakdown
  • Comparison: Include prior period for comparison

Tip: The Balance Sheet provides insight into the company's financial position and solvency.

Cash Flow Statement

Overview

The Cash Flow Statement shows cash inflows and outflows from operating, investing, and financing activities.

Key Features:

  • Separate cash flows by operating, investing, and financing activities
  • Show net increase/decrease in cash for the period
  • Compare with prior period cash flows
  • Include budget versus actual comparisons
  • Provide detailed breakdown of major cash items
  • Export for liquidity analysis

Report Options:

  • Period: Monthly, quarterly, or annual reporting
  • Method: Direct or indirect method
  • Comparison: Compare with prior period or budget
  • Detail Level: Summary or detailed presentation

Tip: Monitor cash flow regularly to ensure sufficient liquidity for operations and obligations.

Unified Reports

Overview

Unified Reports combine multiple financial statements and analytical data in a single comprehensive report.

Key Features:

  • Combine financial statements in one document
  • Include key financial ratios and metrics
  • Provide executive summary and insights
  • Offer customizable layouts and formats
  • Support automated distribution to stakeholders
  • Include graphical representations of key data

Report Components:

  • Financial Statements: Income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow
  • Key Ratios: Liquidity, profitability, and leverage ratios
  • Executive Summary: High-level financial performance
  • Trend Analysis: Historical performance trends
  • Comparative Data: Budget vs. actual and period-over-period

Tip: Use Unified Reports for board meetings and stakeholder presentations for a comprehensive view.

Best Practices

Overview

Follow these best practices to get the most value from your accounting reports.

Regular Review:

  • Review financial reports monthly, even if not required
  • Compare actual results to budget and prior periods
  • Look for unusual fluctuations or trends
  • Investigate significant variances promptly

Distribution:

  • Share relevant reports with appropriate stakeholders
  • Ensure reports are distributed in a timely manner
  • Provide context and explanations for key findings
  • Make reports accessible to those who need them

Analysis:

  • Go beyond just looking at numbers—understand the story behind them
  • Use ratios and metrics to assess performance
  • Compare with industry benchmarks when possible
  • Consider external factors affecting results

Tip: Don't just generate reports—act on the insights they provide to improve your business performance.
