Callback Types

Overview

There are two main types of callbacks in the system, each with specific handling procedures.

Raw Material Return:

  • Description: Unused raw materials returned from production
  • Trigger: Production ends with unused materials
  • Impact: Increases available stock for the returned item
  • Process: Materials are returned to inventory as available stock
  • Stock Effect: Quantity available increases, quantity damaged may increase if materials are degraded

Finished Product Reject:

  • Description: Defective finished products returned from production
  • Trigger: Quality control identifies defective products
  • Impact: Reduces finished goods inventory
  • Process: Products are removed from finished goods inventory
  • Stock Effect: Quantity available decreases, may affect DailyProduce records

Callback Statuses:

  • Pending Approval: Callback awaiting inventory approval
  • Approved by Inventory: Approved, stock updated
  • Completed: Fully processed and closed
  • Rejected: Inventory rejected, no stock change

Tip: Accurate classification of callback types is important for proper stock management and reporting.

Creating Callbacks

Process Overview

Creating callbacks involves recording the return of unused materials or defective products from production.

Required Information:

  • Source Type: Raw material return or finished product reject
  • Item/Product: The specific item or product being returned
  • Quantity: Amount being returned
  • Reason: Explanation for the callback
  • Shift: Production shift during which the callback occurred
  • Callback Time: When the callback was recorded

Callback Creation Process:

  1. Access the callback creation interface
  2. Select the callback type (raw material return or finished product reject)
  3. Choose the specific item or product being returned
  4. Enter the quantity being returned
  5. Provide a detailed reason for the callback
  6. Select the production shift
  7. Record the callback time
  8. Submit the callback for approval

Validation:

  • System validates that the selected item/product exists
  • Ensures quantity is positive and not zero
  • Verifies that the shift is valid and active
  • Checks that all required fields are filled

Important: Callbacks must be submitted for inventory approval before any stock changes occur.

Approving Callbacks

Process Overview

Approving callbacks authorizes the return of materials or products and triggers stock updates.

Approval Process:

  1. Callback is submitted for approval
  2. Inventory team reviews the callback details
  3. Verify the reason and quantity are appropriate
  4. Approve the callback to process the return
  5. System updates stock levels based on callback type
  6. Callback status changes to "approved by inventory"

Approval Validation:

  • Checks that callback can be approved (status is pending)
  • Verifies employee has permission to approve callbacks
  • Ensures callback details are valid
  • Confirms item/product exists in the system

Post-Approval Actions:

  • For raw material returns: Stock levels are updated
  • For finished product rejects: Inventory is reduced
  • Stock movement records are created
  • Related production records may be updated
  • Status changes to "approved by inventory"

Best Practice: Review callback reasons to identify potential process improvements in production.

Rejecting Callbacks

Process Overview

Rejecting callbacks prevents the return of materials or products and maintains current stock levels.

Rejection Process:

  1. Inventory team reviews the callback details
  2. Determine that the callback should not be processed
  3. Open the rejection modal
  4. Provide a reason for the rejection
  5. Confirm the rejection
  6. Callback status changes to "rejected"
  7. No stock changes occur

Rejection Scenarios:

  • Callback quantity seems excessive
  • Reason for callback is unclear or inappropriate
  • Item/product information is incorrect
  • Production shift information is invalid
  • Callback violates company policies

Rejection Validation:

  • Checks that callback can be rejected (status is pending)
  • Verifies employee has permission to reject callbacks
  • Ensures a reason is provided for the rejection
  • Confirms callback details are valid before rejection

Important: Always provide a clear reason when rejecting a callback to help production teams understand the issue.

Completing Callbacks

Process Overview

Completing callbacks finalizes the process after inventory approval and marks it as fully processed.

Completion Process:

  1. Callback must be in "approved by inventory" status
  2. Inventory team confirms all actions are completed
  3. Physical materials/products have been properly handled
  4. Complete the callback to finalize the process
  5. Status changes to "completed"
  6. Callback is closed and archived

Completion Requirements:

  • Callback must be approved by inventory first
  • All physical handling must be completed
  • Related documentation must be finalized
  • Any required follow-up actions must be completed

Post-Completion:

  • Callback is marked as completed
  • No further actions can be taken on the callback
  • Records are maintained for audit purposes
  • Statistics are updated to reflect completion

Tip: Complete callbacks promptly after approval to maintain accurate records and free up system resources.

Stock Impact

Overview

Callbacks have different impacts on stock levels depending on the callback type.

Raw Material Return Impact:

  • Available Stock: Increases by the returned quantity
  • Damaged Stock: May increase if materials are degraded
  • Reserved Stock: No direct impact
  • Stock Movement: Recorded as "callback" type movement
  • Cost Impact: Maintains inventory value

Finished Product Reject Impact:

  • Available Stock: Decreases by the rejected quantity
  • Damaged Stock: May increase depending on handling
  • Production Records: DailyProduce records updated
  • Callback Quantity: Increases in production records
  • Cost Impact: May affect production costing

Stock Movement Tracking:

  • All callback-related stock changes are recorded as movements
  • Movements are linked to the original callback record
  • Provides complete audit trail for all changes
  • Enables reconciliation and reporting
  • Supports inventory accuracy

Best Practice: Regularly review callback impacts on stock levels to identify trends and optimize production processes.

Managing Callbacks

Process Overview

Once callbacks are created, you can view details, track status, and manage related activities.

Viewing Callback Details:

  • Click on a callback in the list to view detailed information
  • See the item or product involved
  • Review the quantity and reason for the callback
  • Check the production shift information
  • View the status and approval information

Callback Management Activities:

  • Status Tracking: Monitor the progress of callbacks
  • Reason Analysis: Identify common callback reasons
  • Volume Monitoring: Track callback volumes over time
  • Process Improvement: Use data to improve production

Actions Available:

  • View Details: See complete callback information
  • Approve Callback: Authorize the callback and update stock
  • Reject Callback: Deny the callback request
  • Complete Callback: Finalize the callback process
  • Export: Download callback data in Excel or CSV

Important: Only approved callbacks result in stock changes. Rejected callbacks leave stock levels unchanged.

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Callbacks features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to create, approve, reject, and complete callbacks
  • Inventory Manager: Can approve, reject, and complete callbacks
  • Production Manager: Can create callbacks and view status
  • Production Staff: Can create callbacks for their shifts
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Only authorized personnel can approve or reject callbacks
  • Production staff can only create callbacks for their shifts
  • Higher-level roles can override lower-level decisions
  • Some actions may require additional authorization

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for callback management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Callback Management

Follow these best practices to maintain efficient and effective callback management.

Documentation:

  • Provide detailed reasons for all callbacks
  • Include specific information about why materials/products were returned
  • Document any patterns or recurring issues
  • Maintain records for audit purposes

Process Efficiency:

  • Process callbacks promptly to maintain accurate stock levels
  • Review callback patterns to identify process improvements
  • Coordinate with production teams to minimize callbacks
  • Complete callbacks in a timely manner after approval

Quality Management:

  • Use callback data to identify quality issues in production
  • Track reasons for finished product rejects
  • Implement corrective actions based on callback trends
  • Monitor raw material return rates for efficiency

Communication:

  • Share callback data with production teams
  • Provide feedback on rejected callbacks
  • Coordinate with quality assurance teams
  • Report trends to management for decision-making

Pro Tip: Regular analysis of callback data can reveal valuable insights for improving production efficiency and reducing waste.
