Creating Health Checks

Process Overview

Creating health checks involves recording the condition assessment of inventory items along with observations and required actions.

Required Information:

  • Item Selection: Choose the inventory item to assess
  • Check Date: Date when the health check was performed
  • Condition: Select the current health condition of the item
  • Quantity Affected: Number of units affected by the condition
  • Observations: Detailed notes about the item's condition
  • Action Taken: Steps taken to address the issue

Steps to Create a Health Check:

  1. Navigate to the Health Checks section
  2. Click the "Add New Health Check" button
  3. Select the item from the inventory list
  4. Set the check date (defaults to today)
  5. Choose the appropriate health condition
  6. Enter the quantity affected (if applicable)
  7. Record your observations about the item's condition
  8. Document any actions taken to address the issue
  9. Click "Save" to record the health check

Quantity Validation:

  • The system validates that the quantity affected does not exceed available stock
  • If quantity affected is greater than available stock, an error will be shown
  • Ensure accurate quantity entries to maintain stock integrity

Tip: Be specific in your observations to help others understand the exact condition of the item. Include details like specific damage, storage conditions, or environmental factors.

Health Conditions

Understanding Health Status

Health conditions represent the current state of inventory items and help prioritize actions.

Health Condition Categories:

  • Good: Item in excellent condition, ready for use or sale
  • Fair: Item usable but showing signs of wear or minor issues
  • Poor: Item needs attention or repair, limited usability
  • Damaged: Item is damaged but may be repairable or reusable for parts
  • Expired: Item has exceeded its shelf life or expiration date

Condition Guidelines:

  • Good: No visible defects, within expiration dates, properly stored
  • Fair: Minor cosmetic issues, slight wear, still functional
  • Poor: Noticeable defects, reduced functionality, approaching end of life
  • Damaged: Physical damage affecting usability, safety concerns
  • Expired: Past expiration date, potential safety risks

Impact of Health Conditions:

  • Health conditions affect item availability for use or sale
  • Items with poor, damaged, or expired conditions may be quarantined
  • Health status influences reorder decisions
  • Conditions are reflected in inventory reports and analytics

Important: Items marked as "Damaged" or "Expired" should be immediately quarantined and not used in production or sales until properly dispositioned.

Managing Health Checks

Process Overview

Once health checks are created, you can view details, track trends, and manage related activities.

Viewing Health Check Details:

  • Click on a health check in the list to view detailed information
  • See the item name, SKU, and category
  • Review the condition assessment and date
  • Check the quantity affected
  • Read detailed observations and actions taken

Health Check Management Activities:

  • Trend Analysis: Identify recurring issues with specific items
  • Pattern Recognition: Spot trends in health conditions
  • Performance Tracking: Monitor improvement or deterioration
  • Compliance Reporting: Generate reports for audits

Health Check Lifecycle:

  • Health checks are created when items are assessed
  • They remain in the system for historical reference
  • They contribute to the overall health status of items
  • They can be reviewed and analyzed over time

Best Practice: Regular health checks help identify issues early, preventing larger problems and reducing waste.

Tracking Actions

Process Overview

Documenting actions taken during health checks ensures accountability and helps track resolution of issues.

Action Documentation:

  • Immediate Actions: Steps taken during the inspection
  • Corrective Measures: Actions to address identified issues
  • Preventive Measures: Steps to prevent future occurrences
  • Disposal Actions: Proper handling of damaged or expired items

Common Actions:

  • Quarantine damaged items
  • Relocate items to appropriate storage areas
  • Initiate disposal process for expired items
  • Request repairs or maintenance
  • Adjust storage conditions
  • Notify relevant stakeholders

Action Tracking Benefits:

  • Ensures accountability for health check findings
  • Tracks resolution of identified issues
  • Provides evidence of compliance with quality standards
  • Helps identify recurring problems
  • Supports continuous improvement initiatives

Tip: Document not just what was done, but also who performed the action and when. This creates a complete audit trail.

Filters and Search

Overview

The Health Checks interface includes filtering and search capabilities to help you find specific assessments quickly.

Available Filters:

  • Search: Search by item name or SKU
  • Condition Filter: Filter by health condition (good, fair, poor, damaged, expired)
  • Date Range: Filter by check date range
  • Action Taken Filter: Filter by whether actions were taken

Using Filters:

  1. Use the search bar to find health checks by item name or SKU
  2. Select a condition from the dropdown to filter by health status
  3. Set date ranges to see checks performed during specific periods
  4. Filter by action taken to see resolved or unresolved issues
  5. Combine multiple filters for more specific results

Additional Features:

  • Sort health checks by clicking on column headers
  • Adjust the number of checks displayed per page
  • Export filtered results to Excel or CSV
  • View summary statistics for the filtered results

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Health Checks features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to create, view, and manage health checks
  • Quality Manager: Can perform health checks and manage quality processes
  • Inventory Manager: Can view and create health checks
  • Quality Inspector: Dedicated role for performing health checks
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Only authorized personnel should perform health checks
  • Training may be required for accurate condition assessments
  • Higher-level roles can review and validate checks from lower-level users

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for health check tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Health Check Management

Follow these best practices to maintain effective health check management.

Assessment Standards:

  • Develop clear criteria for each health condition category
  • Train staff on consistent assessment methods
  • Use standardized terminology in observations
  • Document specific examples of each condition level

Frequency Guidelines:

  • Perform health checks on high-risk items more frequently
  • Check items approaching expiration dates weekly
  • Inspect items in harsh storage conditions more often
  • Review items with previous health issues regularly

Documentation:

  • Be specific and objective in health condition assessments
  • Include quantitative measurements when possible
  • Document the location where the item was found
  • Record environmental conditions during assessment

Follow-up Actions:

  • Immediately quarantine items with critical health issues
  • Follow established protocols for disposal of expired items
  • Track resolution of identified issues
  • Share findings with relevant teams for process improvements

Pro Tip: Use the analytics and reporting features to identify trends in health conditions and improve preventive measures.
