Stock Categories

Understanding Stock Types

Stock is categorized into different types to provide accurate visibility into inventory status and availability.

Stock Categories:

  • Available Stock: Quantity ready for use or sale
  • Reserved Stock: Quantity allocated for specific purposes (production, orders, etc.)
  • Damaged Stock: Quantity that is unusable due to damage or defects
  • Total Stock: Sum of all stock categories (Available + Reserved + Damaged)

Stock Calculations:

  • Available for Sale/Use: Available Stock - Reserved Stock
  • Effective Stock: Available Stock (what can actually be used)
  • Unavailable Stock: Reserved + Damaged Stock

Stock Movement Tracking:

  • All stock changes are recorded in the stock movement log
  • Each movement includes type (in, out, adjustment), quantity, and reason
  • Reference to the transaction that caused the movement
  • Date and time of the movement

Tip: Regularly reconcile your physical stock counts with system records to ensure accuracy. Discrepancies should be investigated and documented.

Managing Stock

Process Overview

Managing stock involves monitoring levels, updating quantities, and ensuring accurate records.

Stock Management Activities:

  • Monitoring: Regularly check stock levels and health status
  • Updating: Adjust quantities based on physical counts or transactions
  • Tracking: Follow stock movements and changes
  • Alerting: Respond to low stock and critical health alerts

Stock Management Process:

  1. Regularly review stock levels in the system
  2. Perform physical stock counts periodically
  3. Compare physical counts with system records
  4. Make necessary adjustments through the approval process
  5. Monitor and respond to alerts and notifications
  6. Review and update reorder levels as needed

Stock Visibility:

  • Dashboard displays critical stock information
  • Low stock items are highlighted for attention
  • Health status indicators show item conditions
  • Recent stock movements are displayed

Best Practice: Establish a regular schedule for stock counts and stick to it. Consistency is key to maintaining accurate inventory records.

Health Monitoring

Process Overview

Health monitoring tracks the condition of inventory items to ensure quality and safety.

Health Status Categories:

  • Good: Item in excellent condition, ready for use
  • Warning: Item showing signs of deterioration, monitor closely
  • Critical: Item approaching expiry or significantly deteriorated
  • Expired: Item has exceeded its shelf life or expiration date

Health Factors:

  • Expiration Dates: Track when items expire and need replacement
  • Physical Condition: Monitor for damage, spoilage, or deterioration
  • Storage Conditions: Ensure proper temperature, humidity, and handling
  • Rotation: Follow FIFO (First In, First Out) principles

Health Monitoring Process:

  1. Regularly inspect items for signs of deterioration
  2. Update health status in the system as needed
  3. Move expired or critical items to appropriate areas
  4. Dispose of expired items according to company policy
  5. Document any health-related issues
  6. Review health reports to identify trends

Important: Items with critical or expired health status should be quarantined immediately and not used in production or sales.

Stock Adjustments

Process Overview

Stock adjustments allow you to update quantities when discrepancies are found between physical counts and system records.

When to Make Adjustments:

  • After physical stock counts reveal discrepancies
  • When items are damaged during handling or storage
  • To correct data entry errors
  • When items are lost or stolen
  • To account for shrinkage or evaporation
  • When items expire and need to be written off

Stock Adjustment Process:

  1. Identify the item that needs adjustment
  2. Click the "Edit Stock" button in the stock list
  3. Enter the correct quantities for available, reserved, and damaged stock
  4. Update health status if necessary
  5. Enter any relevant notes about the adjustment
  6. Click "Save" to submit the adjustment
  7. If you're a non-super admin, provide a reason and submit for approval

Adjustment Considerations:

  • Always investigate the cause of significant adjustments
  • Document the reason for each adjustment
  • Large adjustments may require additional approval
  • Adjustments affect inventory valuation
  • All adjustments create a stock movement record

Caution: Frequent or large stock adjustments may indicate underlying issues with inventory management processes that should be investigated.

Reorder Alerts

Process Overview

Reorder alerts notify you when stock levels fall below predetermined thresholds, helping prevent stockouts.

How Reorder Alerts Work:

  • Each item has a configurable reorder level
  • When available stock falls below this level, an alert is triggered
  • Low stock items are highlighted in the dashboard
  • Reorder alerts appear in the critical alerts section

Setting Reorder Levels:

  • Reorder levels are set when creating or updating items
  • Consider lead times when setting reorder levels
  • Factor in usage patterns and seasonal variations
  • Review and adjust reorder levels regularly
  • Set different levels for critical vs. non-critical items

Reorder Process:

  1. Monitor alerts in the dashboard for low stock items
  2. Review the suggested reorder quantities
  3. Create purchase orders for items that need replenishment
  4. Consider bundling multiple items from the same supplier
  5. Submit purchase orders for approval if required
  6. Track the status of orders until delivery

Best Practice: Set reorder levels slightly higher than minimum to account for unexpected demand increases or supply delays.

Filters and Search

Overview

The Stock Levels interface includes filtering and search capabilities to help you find specific items quickly.

Available Filters:

  • Search: Search by item name or SKU
  • Category Filter: Filter by item category (raw material, packaging, consumable, equipment)
  • Status Filter: Filter by stock status (low stock, overstock)
  • Health Status Filter: Filter by health status (good, warning, critical, expired)
  • Date Range: Filter by last stock take date

Using Filters:

  1. Use the search bar to find items by name or SKU
  2. Select a category from the dropdown to filter by item type
  3. Use the status filter to find low stock or overstocked items
  4. Filter by health status to identify items needing attention
  5. Set date ranges to see recently updated stock records
  6. Combine multiple filters for more specific results

Additional Features:

  • Sort stock by clicking on column headers
  • Adjust the number of items displayed per page
  • Export filtered results to Excel or CSV
  • View summary statistics for the filtered results

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Stock Levels features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to view, update, and adjust stock levels directly
  • Inventory Manager: Can manage stock but requires approval for adjustments
  • Stock Controller: Limited access to view and request stock changes
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Stock adjustments require approval for non-super admin users
  • Higher-level roles can approve stock changes from lower-level users
  • View-only permissions allow monitoring without modification rights
  • Some actions may be restricted based on item categories

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for stock management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Stock Management

Follow these best practices to maintain accurate and efficient stock management.

Stock Counting:

  • Establish a regular schedule for physical stock counts
  • Count high-value items more frequently
  • Investigate and document discrepancies
  • Use cycle counting for continuous verification

Reorder Management:

  • Set reorder levels based on lead times and usage patterns
  • Consider seasonal variations in demand
  • Review and adjust reorder levels regularly
  • Keep safety stock for critical items

Quality Control:

  • Regularly inspect items for quality and condition
  • Follow FIFO (First In, First Out) principles
  • Quarantine damaged or expired items immediately
  • Train staff on proper handling and storage techniques

Documentation:

  • Document reasons for all stock adjustments
  • Keep records of physical counts and discrepancies
  • Maintain logs of damaged or expired items
  • Track supplier performance related to stock quality

Pro Tip: Use the analytics and reporting features to identify trends and optimize your stock management processes over time.
