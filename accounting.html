Chart of Accounts

Overview

The Chart of Accounts organizes and classifies all financial accounts in the system.

Key Features:

  • Organize accounts by type (assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses)
  • Assign unique account codes for identification
  • Define account hierarchies and sub-accounts
  • Track account balances and activity
  • Link accounts to specific departments or cost centers
  • Control account posting permissions

Account Categories:

  • Assets: Resources owned by the company (cash, inventory, equipment)
  • Liabilities: Obligations owed to others (loans, payables)
  • Equity: Owner's interest in the company (capital, retained earnings)
  • Revenue: Income from business operations (sales, services)
  • Expenses: Costs incurred in business operations (salaries, rent)

Account Management:

  • Create new accounts with proper classification
  • Modify existing account properties
  • Inactivate accounts that are no longer needed
  • View account history and transactions
  • Export account lists for reporting
  • Maintain account hierarchies

Accounting Periods

Overview

Accounting Periods define the time frames for financial reporting and transaction posting.

Key Features:

  • Define fiscal years and reporting periods
  • Control when transactions can be posted
  • Close periods for financial reporting
  • Reopen closed periods when necessary
  • Track period status (open, closed, locked)
  • Manage period-end processes

Period Management:

  • Create new accounting periods
  • Set opening and closing dates
  • Define period types (monthly, quarterly, annually)
  • Control posting access by period
  • Close periods for financial reporting
  • Reopen periods for corrections when needed

Period Statuses:

  • Open: Transactions can be posted normally
  • Closed: No new postings allowed
  • Locked: Period is closed and cannot be reopened
  • Adjusting: Special period for adjusting entries

Journal Entries

Overview

Journal Entries record all financial transactions in the general ledger.

Key Features:

  • Record debit and credit transactions
  • Ensure accounting equation balance
  • Link to supporting documentation
  • Track transaction dates and references
  • Assign to specific accounting periods
  • Review and approve entries before posting

Entry Components:

  • Entry Date: Date of the transaction
  • Reference: Transaction reference number
  • Account: General ledger account affected
  • Debit/Credit: Amount and direction of the entry
  • Description: Explanation of the transaction
  • Posting Status: Whether entry is posted

Entry Types:

  • Standard Entry: Regular business transactions
  • Adjusting Entry: Period-end adjustments
  • Reversing Entry: Reverse previous entries
  • Closing Entry: Close temporary accounts
  • Compound Entry: Multiple debits and credits

Posting Status

Overview

Posting Status tracks the status of journal entries as they move through the posting process.

Key Features:

  • Track entry status (draft, pending, posted, rejected)
  • Monitor batch posting processes
  • Identify entries requiring attention
  • Review posting errors and exceptions
  • Retry failed postings
  • Generate posting reports

Status Types:

  • Draft: Entry is being prepared
  • Pending: Entry awaiting posting
  • Posted: Entry successfully posted to ledger
  • Rejected: Entry failed posting validation
  • Processing: Entry currently being posted

Posting Management:

  • Review pending entries
  • Post entries individually or in batches
  • Identify and resolve posting errors
  • Retry failed postings
  • Monitor posting progress
  • Generate posting status reports

Bank Reconciliation

Overview

Bank Reconciliation compares bank statements with internal records to ensure accuracy.

Key Features:

  • Compare bank statement balances with book balances
  • Identify outstanding deposits and checks
  • Track bank charges and interest
  • Resolve discrepancies between records
  • Document reconciliation adjustments
  • Generate reconciliation reports

Reconciliation Process:

  • Obtain bank statement for the period
  • Compare ending balances
  • Identify outstanding deposits
  • Identify outstanding checks
  • Account for bank charges and interest
  • Prepare reconciliation adjustments
  • Verify balances match after adjustments

Reconciliation Benefits:

  • Ensure accuracy of financial records
  • Identify unauthorized transactions
  • Discover timing differences
  • Validate internal controls
  • Prepare for audits
  • Maintain financial integrity

Reports

Overview

Accounting Reports provide comprehensive financial statements and analytical reports.

Available Reports:

  • General Ledger: Detailed account transactions and balances
  • Trial Balance: Account balances to verify debits equal credits
  • Income Statement: Revenue, expenses, and profit/loss
  • Balance Sheet: Assets, liabilities, and equity position
  • Cash Flow Statement: Cash inflows and outflows by activity
  • Unified Reports: Comprehensive integrated financial reports

Report Features:

  • Customizable date ranges and filters
  • Export to Excel and PDF formats
  • Visual charts and graphs for better understanding
  • Detailed drill-down capabilities
  • Comparative reporting across periods
  • Automated report generation

Report Usage:

  • Financial statement preparation
  • Management decision making
  • Regulatory compliance
  • Investor and stakeholder reporting
  • Internal financial analysis
  • Auditing and verification
