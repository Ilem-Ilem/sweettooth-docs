Dispatch Process

Process Overview

The dispatch process involves approving requests and then releasing items to requesting departments.

Dispatch Workflow:

  1. Item request is created by a department
  2. Request is submitted for approval
  3. Inventory team reviews and approves the request
  4. Items are reserved from available stock
  5. Store keeper accesses the dispatch module
  6. Approved items are dispatched to the requesting department
  7. Stock levels are automatically updated

Prerequisites for Dispatch:

  • Request must be in "approved" status
  • Sufficient stock must be available
  • Items must be properly reserved
  • Authorized personnel must perform the dispatch

Dispatch Validation:

  • System checks for sufficient stock before dispatch
  • Validates that items are properly reserved
  • Ensures dispatch quantities do not exceed approved amounts
  • Verifies department has permission to receive items

Tip: Always verify that items are physically handed over to the requesting department and obtain acknowledgment when possible.

Approving Items

Process Overview

Approving items in a request reserves them from available stock and prepares them for dispatch.

Approval Process:

  1. Access the dispatch module and open the request
  2. Review the items and quantities requested
  3. Verify sufficient stock is available
  4. Approve all items or specific quantities
  5. Click "Approve Items" to reserve them
  6. Approved items are now reserved and ready for dispatch

Approval Options:

  • Approve All: Approve all requested quantities
  • Partial Approval: Approve specific quantities for each item
  • Approve Individually: Approve items one by one

Approval Validation:

  • System checks for sufficient stock before approval
  • Approves only quantities that are available
  • Blocks approval if stock is insufficient
  • Updates item status to "approved" after approval

Important: Items must be approved before they can be dispatched. Approval reserves the items from available stock.

Dispatching Items

Process Overview

Dispatching items releases them from inventory and updates stock levels accordingly.

Dispatch Process:

  1. Open the dispatch modal for an approved request
  2. Verify that items have been approved
  3. Confirm the quantities to be dispatched
  4. Click "Dispatch Items" to release them
  5. Stock levels are automatically updated
  6. Request status is updated to reflect dispatch

Dispatch Validation:

  • System verifies that items are properly approved
  • Confirms sufficient approved quantities are available
  • Checks that items are reserved for dispatch
  • Validates that dispatch quantities do not exceed approved amounts

Post-Dispatch Actions:

  • Stock levels are reduced by dispatched quantities
  • Reserved quantities are released
  • Stock movement records are created
  • Request status is updated (completed or partially dispatched)
  • Audit trail is updated with dispatch details

Best Practice: Always verify that the physical items match the quantities being dispatched before confirming the transaction.

Stock Movements

Process Overview

Each dispatch creates a stock movement record that tracks the flow of inventory.

Stock Movement Details:

  • Type: "Out" for dispatched items
  • Quantity: Negative value representing dispatched amount
  • Before/After: Stock levels before and after the movement
  • Reference: Link to the original request
  • Personnel: Who performed the dispatch
  • Date/Time: When the dispatch occurred
  • Notes: Description of the dispatch activity

Movement Tracking:

  • All dispatches create movement records automatically
  • Movements are linked to the original request
  • Historical tracking enables audit trails
  • Movements help with inventory reconciliation
  • Reports can be generated based on movement data

Movement Impact:

  • Reduces available stock by dispatched quantities
  • Updates item availability for other requests
  • Provides visibility into inventory flow
  • Supports cost accounting and valuation
  • Enables trend analysis and forecasting

Tip: Regularly review stock movements to identify patterns and optimize inventory management processes.

Managing Dispatches

Process Overview

Once dispatches are processed, you can view details, track status, and manage related activities.

Viewing Dispatch Details:

  • Click on a request in the list to view dispatch details
  • See all items included in the dispatch
  • View quantities requested, approved, and dispatched
  • Check the status of the dispatch
  • Review the responsible personnel

Dispatch Management Activities:

  • Status Tracking: Monitor the progress of dispatches
  • Quantity Monitoring: Track dispatched vs. requested quantities
  • Reconciliation: Verify physical vs. system quantities
  • Reporting: Generate dispatch reports for analysis

Actions Available:

  • View Details: See complete dispatch information
  • Approve Items: Reserve items for dispatch
  • Dispatch Items: Release items to requesting department
  • Export: Download dispatch data in Excel or CSV

Important: Once items are dispatched, the transaction is typically final. Verify quantities before confirming dispatch.

Filters and Search

Overview

The Item Dispatches interface includes filtering and search capabilities to help you find specific dispatches quickly.

Available Filters:

  • Search: Search by request number, department name, or requester name
  • Shift Filter: Filter by production shift
  • Date Range: Filter by dispatch date range

Using Filters:

  1. Use the search bar to find dispatches by request number or department
  2. Select a shift from the dropdown to filter by production shift
  3. Set date ranges to see dispatches during specific periods
  4. Combine multiple filters for more specific results

Additional Features:

  • Sort dispatches by clicking on column headers
  • Adjust the number of dispatches displayed per page
  • Export filtered results to Excel or CSV
  • View dispatch summary statistics

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Item Dispatches features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to approve and dispatch items
  • Inventory Manager: Can approve and dispatch items
  • Store Keeper: Can view and process dispatches
  • Department Head: Can view dispatches for their department
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Only authorized personnel can approve and dispatch items
  • Higher-level roles can override lower-level decisions
  • Some departments may have restricted access to certain items
  • Financial approval may be required for high-value items

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for dispatch management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Dispatch Management

Follow these best practices to maintain efficient and effective dispatch management.

Verification:

  • Always verify that requested items match physical inventory
  • Confirm quantities before approving and dispatching
  • Check item condition before dispatch
  • Verify recipient department and authorization

Documentation:

  • Keep detailed records of all dispatch activities
  • Obtain acknowledgment from recipients when possible
  • Document any discrepancies or issues
  • Maintain logs of approved and dispatched items

Efficiency:

  • Process dispatches in batches when possible
  • Coordinate with requesting departments for optimal timing
  • Use the "Approve All" function for complete requests
  • Regularly review pending requests to prevent delays

Communication:

  • Notify requesting departments when items are dispatched
  • Provide status updates throughout the process
  • Coordinate with logistics for item delivery
  • Address any issues or concerns promptly

Pro Tip: Regularly review dispatch patterns to identify opportunities for process improvements and efficiency gains.
