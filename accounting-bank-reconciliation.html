Reconciliation Process

Overview

The bank reconciliation process compares bank statements with internal records.

Reconciliation Steps:

  1. Obtain bank statement for the period
  2. Compare ending balances
  3. Identify outstanding deposits
  4. Identify outstanding checks
  5. Account for bank charges and interest
  6. Prepare reconciliation adjustments
  7. Verify balances match after adjustments
  8. Document the reconciliation

Key Elements:

  • Bank Balance: Balance per bank statement
  • Book Balance: Balance per company records
  • Outstanding Deposits: Deposits made but not yet reflected in bank
  • Outstanding Checks: Checks written but not yet cleared
  • Bank Errors: Errors made by the bank
  • Company Errors: Errors in company records

Reconciliation Formula:

Bank Balance + Outstanding Deposits - Outstanding Checks = Book Balance + Bank Charges - Bank Credits

Tip: Perform bank reconciliations monthly to catch discrepancies early and maintain accurate records.

Preparing for Reconciliation

Overview

Proper preparation ensures a smooth reconciliation process.

Preparation Steps:

  1. Obtain the bank statement for the period
  2. Ensure all transactions are recorded in the system
  3. Verify that all deposits are recorded
  4. Confirm that all checks are recorded
  5. Identify any bank charges or interest
  6. Prepare a list of outstanding items
  7. Verify the beginning balance matches
  8. Ensure the accounting period is correct

Required Information:

  • Bank Statement: Official statement from the bank
  • Internal Records: Company's cash account records
  • Deposit Slips: Documentation of deposits made
  • Check Registers: Record of checks issued
  • Bank Notices: Notifications of charges or credits
  • Previous Reconciliation: Prior period reconciliation

Preparation Checklist:

  • All deposits recorded in the books
  • All checks issued recorded in the books
  • Bank charges and fees identified
  • Interest earned recorded
  • Beginning balances verified
  • Accounting period confirmed

Best Practice: Maintain a standardized preparation checklist to ensure all necessary steps are completed.

Identifying Differences

Overview

Identifying differences between bank and book records is a key step in reconciliation.

Common Differences:

  • Outstanding Deposits: Deposits made but not yet reflected in bank
  • Outstanding Checks: Checks written but not yet cleared
  • Bank Charges: Fees charged by the bank
  • Interest Earned: Interest credited by the bank
  • NSF Checks: Checks returned for insufficient funds
  • Bank Errors: Mistakes made by the bank
  • Company Errors: Mistakes in company records

Difference Identification Process:

  1. Compare bank statement transactions with book records
  2. Mark items that appear in both records
  3. Identify items that appear in only one record
  4. Group differences by type
  5. Investigate the cause of each difference
  6. Document the reason for each difference

Difference Analysis:

  • Review timing differences
  • Identify potential errors
  • Check for missing transactions
  • Verify bank charges and fees
  • Confirm interest earnings
  • Investigate unusual items

Important: Large or unusual differences should be investigated immediately to identify potential fraud or errors.

Resolving Differences

Overview

Resolving differences involves correcting errors and documenting adjustments.

Resolving Timing Differences:

  • Outstanding Deposits: These will clear in the next period
  • Outstanding Checks: These will clear when presented to the bank
  • Bank Charges: Record these in the books if not already recorded
  • Interest Earned: Record these in the books if not already recorded

Resolving Errors:

  1. Identify the type of error (bank or company)
  2. Determine the correct amount
  3. Make necessary corrections
  4. Record any required journal entries
  5. Document the correction
  6. Verify the correction

Adjustment Recording:

  • Record bank charges not previously recorded
  • Record interest earned not previously recorded
  • Correct any book errors
  • Reverse any incorrect entries
  • Make adjusting entries as needed
  • Update account balances

Tip: Always document the reason for adjustments and maintain supporting documentation for audit purposes.

Completing Reconciliation

Overview

Completing the reconciliation involves final verification and documentation.

Final Verification:

  1. Verify that all items are accounted for
  2. Confirm that adjusted balances match
  3. Review all adjustments made
  4. Verify that journal entries are correct
  5. Ensure all supporting documentation is attached
  6. Obtain necessary approvals

Documentation Requirements:

  • Completed reconciliation worksheet
  • Supporting documentation for adjustments
  • Journal entries for adjustments
  • Bank statement copies
  • Management approval signatures
  • Reconciliation date and preparer information

Post-Reconciliation Activities:

  • Update general ledger accounts
  • File reconciliation documentation
  • Prepare reconciliation reports
  • Notify management of significant findings
  • Update internal controls as needed
  • Begin preparation for next period

Best Practice: Retain reconciliation documentation for at least seven years for audit and tax purposes.

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Bank Reconciliation features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to all reconciliation features
  • Accounting Manager: Can perform and approve reconciliations
  • Accountant: Can perform reconciliations
  • Bookkeeper: Can view reconciliation status
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Only authorized personnel should perform reconciliations
  • Segregation of duties should be maintained
  • Approval requirements may apply to adjustments
  • Some accounts may have restricted access
  • Access to sensitive bank information may be limited

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for reconciliation tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Bank Reconciliation

Follow these best practices to maintain effective bank reconciliation processes.

Frequency:

  • Perform reconciliations monthly
  • Consider more frequent reconciliations for high-volume accounts
  • Reconcile immediately after receiving bank statements
  • Don't delay reconciliation beyond the statement period
  • Perform reconciliations consistently
  • Align reconciliation timing with business cycles

Accuracy:

  • Verify all transactions before starting reconciliation
  • Double-check all calculations
  • Investigate all discrepancies
  • Document all adjustments thoroughly
  • Retain all supporting documentation
  • Have reconciliations reviewed by supervisors

Controls:

  • Maintain segregation of duties
  • Implement approval controls for adjustments
  • Regularly review access permissions
  • Monitor for unusual transactions
  • Implement automated validation controls
  • Conduct periodic audits

Documentation:

  • Maintain detailed reconciliation worksheets
  • Keep copies of bank statements
  • Document all adjustments made
  • Record reconciliation dates and preparers
  • Train staff on proper procedures
  • Regularly update documentation

Important: Bank reconciliation is a critical internal control. Maintain strict procedures and document all activities for audit purposes.
