Employee Appraisals

The Employee Appraisals system enables managers to conduct performance evaluations, track employee development, and maintain comprehensive performance records.

Overview

The Employee Appraisals system facilitates the performance evaluation process:

Performance Reviews

Conduct structured performance evaluations for employees

Development Planning

Identify strengths, areas for improvement, and future goals

Accessing Employee Appraisals

  1. Log in to the system with appropriate credentials (typically managers or HR personnel)
  2. Navigate to the Branch Dashboard
  3. Click on "Employee Appraisals" in the sidebar menu under the Organization section
  4. The Employee Appraisals page will display a list of employees eligible for review

Employee Appraisal List

The main page displays employees available for appraisal:

Information Displayed:

  • Employee Name - Full name of the employee
  • Email - Contact email address
  • Department - Department the employee belongs to
  • Roles - Assigned roles and responsibilities
  • Last Review Date - Date of the most recent performance review
  • Current Rating - Overall performance rating

Actions Available:

  • Start Appraisal - (the button may appear as text, a star icon, or similar depending on your interface) Click to begin the appraisal process for an employee

Conducting an Employee Appraisal

The appraisal process involves evaluating employee performance across multiple dimensions:

Starting an Appraisal:

  1. Find the employee in the appraisal list
  2. Click the "Start Appraisal" button (the button may appear as text, a star icon, or similar depending on your interface) for that employee
  3. The system will check if an appraisal already exists for the current cycle
  4. If no current appraisal exists, you'll be directed to the appraisal form

Appraisal Form Fields:

  • Rating - (dropdown/slider) Select a performance rating (typically 1-5 scale)
  • Comments - (textarea) Provide overall performance comments (required)
  • Strengths - (textarea) Document the employee's key strengths (required)
  • Areas for Improvement - (textarea) Identify areas needing development (required)
  • Goals - (textarea) Set objectives for the next review period (required)

Appraisal Cycle Selection:

  • Select Cycle - (dropdown) Choose the appropriate appraisal cycle for this review
  • Cycle Information - View details about the selected cycle including dates and requirements

Submitting the Appraisal:

  1. Complete all required fields in the appraisal form
  2. Review the information for accuracy
  3. Click the "Submit Appraisal" button (the button may appear as text, an icon, or similar depending on your interface)
  4. The system will save the appraisal and update the employee's performance rating

Appraisal Stages

The appraisal process may involve multiple stages depending on your organization's setup:

Stages Overview:

  • Draft - Initial stage where appraisal is being prepared
  • Self Review - Employee reviews their own performance
  • Manager Review - Manager evaluates employee performance
  • HR Review - HR validates and calibrates ratings
  • Completed - Finalized appraisal ready for action

Self-Assessment Fields:

  • Accomplishments - (textarea) Employee's self-reported achievements
  • Challenges - (textarea) Obstacles faced during the period
  • Goals Achieved - (textarea) Objectives met during the period
  • Development Needs - (textarea) Areas where employee seeks growth
  • Self Rating - (dropdown/slider) Employee's self-evaluation

Manager Assessment Fields:

  • Manager Rating - (dropdown/slider) Manager's performance rating
  • Manager Comments - (textarea) Manager's observations
  • Strengths - (textarea) Manager's perspective on employee strengths
  • Areas for Improvement - (textarea) Manager's recommendations
  • Goals - (textarea) Manager's expectations for next period
  • Development Plan - (textarea) Specific development activities

HR Assessment Fields:

  • HR Rating - (dropdown/slider) HR's calibrated rating
  • HR Comments - (textarea) HR's observations
  • Calibration Notes - (textarea) HR's calibration rationale
  • Final Decision - (textarea) HR's final determination

Filtering and Searching

Use the filtering options to find specific employees for appraisal:

Search Options:

  • Search Box - (text input) Enter text to search across employee names and emails

Filter Options:

  • Department Filter - (dropdown) Filter by specific department

Employee Performance Tracking

The system maintains historical performance data:

Automatic Updates:

  • Performance Rating - Updated automatically when appraisal is submitted
  • Last Review Date - Updated to current date when appraisal is completed
  • Historical Records - Maintained for trend analysis

Integration with Employee Records:

  • Completed appraisals are linked to employee profiles
  • Performance history is accessible from employee details
  • Development plans are integrated with goal-setting features

Validation and Requirements

The system enforces requirements to ensure comprehensive appraisals:

Required Fields:

  • Rating must be selected (1-5 scale)
  • Comments must be provided (minimum length enforced)
  • Strengths must be documented (minimum length enforced)
  • Areas for improvement must be identified (minimum length enforced)
  • Goals must be set for the next period (minimum length enforced)

Minimum Length Requirements:

  • Comments, strengths, areas for improvement, and goals typically require at least 10 characters
  • These requirements ensure meaningful feedback is provided

Tips and Best Practices

  • Prepare for appraisals by reviewing the employee's past performance and goals
  • Provide specific examples to support your ratings and comments
  • Focus on behaviors and outcomes rather than personality traits
  • Set SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  • Document both positive contributions and areas needing improvement
  • Align goals with departmental and organizational objectives
  • Encourage employee participation in the self-assessment process
  • Follow up on development plans after the appraisal is completed
  • Maintain confidentiality of appraisal discussions
