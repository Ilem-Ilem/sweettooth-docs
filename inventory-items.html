Creating New Items

Process Overview

Creating new items involves filling out a form with essential details. The system automatically generates a unique SKU based on the item name and category.

Required Information:

  • Name: Descriptive name for the item
  • Category: Select from raw material, packaging, consumable, or equipment
  • Unit of Measure: Choose appropriate UOM (kg, liters, pcs, etc.)
  • Reorder Level: Minimum stock level to trigger reorder alerts
  • Status: Active or inactive

Steps to Create an Item:

  1. Navigate to the Items Management section
  2. Click the "Add New Item" button
  3. Fill in the item details in the form
  4. Review the auto-generated SKU (can be modified if needed)
  5. Click "Save" to submit the item
  6. If you're a non-super admin, you'll need to provide a reason for the change and submit for approval

Tip: The system automatically generates SKUs based on the item name and category. For example, a "raw material" named "Sugar" might generate "RM-SUG-0001".

Important: Non-super admin users must submit item creation requests for approval. Super admins can create items directly.

Updating Items

Process Overview

Updating items allows you to modify existing item details. The process varies depending on your user role.

Update Process:

  • Super Admins: Can update items directly without approval
  • Regular Users: Must submit updates for approval with a reason
  • Changes to item details are tracked in the audit log

Steps to Update an Item:

  1. Find the item you want to update in the items list
  2. Click the "Edit" button in the actions column
  3. Modify the item details as needed
  4. Click "Save" to submit the changes
  5. If you're a non-super admin, provide a reason for the change and submit for approval

Updatable Fields:

  • Item name
  • SKU (must remain unique)
  • Category
  • Unit of measure
  • Reorder level
  • Maximum stock level
  • Status (active/inactive)

Caution: Updating item details may affect related records like purchase orders, stock movements, and recipes. Consider the impact before making changes.

Stock Adjustments

Process Overview

Stock adjustments allow you to update the available, reserved, and damaged quantities for an item. This is typically done during stock takes or when correcting discrepancies.

Stock Adjustment Process:

  • Available Stock: Quantity ready for use or sale
  • Reserved Stock: Quantity allocated for specific purposes
  • Damaged Stock: Quantity that is unusable due to damage
  • All adjustments are tracked in the stock movement log

Steps to Adjust Stock:

  1. Find the item in the items list
  2. Click the "Adjust Stock" button in the actions column
  3. Enter the new quantities for available, reserved, and damaged stock
  4. Add any relevant notes about the adjustment
  5. Click "Save" to submit the adjustment
  6. If you're a non-super admin, provide a reason and submit for approval

Stock Adjustment Considerations:

  • Adjustments should be based on physical stock counts
  • Large adjustments may require investigation
  • All adjustments create a stock movement record
  • Adjustments may affect reorder alerts

Best Practice: Perform regular stock takes to ensure accuracy. Document the reason for any significant adjustments.

Deleting Items

Process Overview

Deleting items removes them from your inventory system. This action requires careful consideration as it may affect related records.

Deletion Process:

  • Super Admins: Can delete items directly after confirmation
  • Regular Users: Must submit deletion requests for approval
  • The system shows a summary of related data that will be affected

Data Affected by Deletion:

  • Stock records associated with the item
  • Recipes that use this item as an ingredient
  • Purchase orders containing this item
  • Stock movement records for this item
  • Any related transactions or reports

Steps to Delete an Item:

  1. Find the item in the items list
  2. Click the "Delete" button in the actions column
  3. Review the summary of related data that will be deleted
  4. If you're a non-super admin, provide a reason and submit for approval
  5. If you're a super admin, confirm the deletion in the dialog

Warning: Deletion is irreversible and affects related data. Consider deactivating items instead of deleting them if you might need the historical data later.

Filters and Search

Overview

The Items Management interface includes powerful filtering and search capabilities to help you find specific items quickly.

Available Filters:

  • Search: Search by item name or SKU
  • Category Filter: Filter by item category (raw material, packaging, consumable, equipment)
  • Status Filter: Filter by item status (active, inactive)
  • Stock Level Filter: Filter by stock level (low stock, out of stock)

Using Filters:

  1. Use the search bar to find items by name or SKU
  2. Select a category from the dropdown to filter by category
  3. Select a status to filter by active/inactive items
  4. Use the stock level filter to find items with low stock or out of stock
  5. Combine multiple filters for more specific results

Additional Features:

  • Sort items by clicking on column headers
  • Adjust the number of items displayed per page
  • Export filtered results to Excel or CSV
  • View low stock items highlighted in the dashboard

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Items Management features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to create, update, delete items and adjust stock directly
  • Inventory Manager: Can manage items but requires approval for certain actions
  • Stock Controller: Limited access to view and request changes to items
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Approval Workflow:

  • Non-super admin users must submit changes for approval
  • Item creation, updates, and deletions require approval
  • Stock adjustments require approval for non-super admins
  • Approvals are handled through the Audit Management section

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for inventory management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Item Management

Follow these best practices to maintain accurate and efficient item management.

Item Naming Conventions:

  • Use clear, descriptive names that distinguish items
  • Include relevant specifications in the name if needed
  • Be consistent with naming across similar items

Setting Reorder Levels:

  • Set reorder levels based on lead times and usage patterns
  • Consider seasonal variations in demand
  • Review and adjust reorder levels regularly

Stock Management:

  • Perform regular physical stock counts to verify accuracy
  • Investigate and document significant stock discrepancies
  • Keep damaged stock separate and dispose of appropriately
  • Monitor slow-moving items to prevent obsolescence

Documentation:

  • Provide clear reasons when submitting changes for approval
  • Keep notes updated with relevant information
  • Document any special handling requirements for items

Pro Tip: Regularly review your item list to deactivate items that are no longer in use rather than deleting them to preserve historical data.
