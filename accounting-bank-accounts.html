Creating Bank Accounts

Process Overview

Creating bank accounts involves defining account properties and linking them to general ledger accounts.

Required Information:

  • Account Name: Descriptive name for the bank account
  • Account Number: Bank account number
  • Bank Name: Name of the financial institution
  • Branch Information: Branch name and location
  • Account Type: Checking, savings, credit, etc.
  • GL Account: Linked general ledger account
  • Opening Balance: Initial balance for the account
  • Opening Date: Date the account was opened

Steps to Create a Bank Account:

  1. Navigate to the Bank Accounts section
  2. Click the "Add New Account" button
  3. Enter the account name and description
  4. Provide the bank account number
  5. Select the bank name and branch information
  6. Choose the account type
  7. Link to the appropriate general ledger account
  8. Enter the opening balance and date
  9. Set any additional properties
  10. Click "Save" to create the account

Account Properties:

  • Active Status: Whether the account is currently active
  • Online Banking: Whether online banking is enabled
  • Statement Frequency: How often statements are received
  • Minimum Balance: Required minimum balance
  • Interest Rate: Interest rate if applicable
  • Overdraft Limit: Overdraft protection limit

Tip: Link each bank account to the appropriate general ledger account to ensure accurate financial reporting.

Account Information

Overview

Bank account information includes all details about your banking relationships.

Basic Information:

  • Account Number: Last four digits of the account number
  • Bank Name: Name of the financial institution
  • Branch Name: Name of the specific branch
  • Branch Address: Complete address of the branch
  • Account Type: Checking, savings, credit, etc.
  • Account Status: Active, closed, dormant, etc.

Financial Information:

  • Current Balance: Actual balance in the account
  • Available Balance: Funds available for use
  • Opening Balance: Initial balance when account was created
  • Opening Date: Date the account was opened
  • Currency: Currency in which the account is denominated
  • GL Account: Linked general ledger account

Additional Details:

  • Contact Person: Primary contact at the bank
  • Contact Number: Phone number for the account
  • Online Banking: Online banking access details
  • Statement Frequency: How often statements are received
  • Minimum Balance: Required minimum balance
  • Interest Rate: Interest rate if applicable

Best Practice: Keep bank account information up to date to ensure accurate financial records and smooth operations.

Managing Bank Accounts

Process Overview

Once accounts are created, you can view details, update properties, and manage related activities.

Viewing Account Details:

  • Click on an account in the list to view detailed information
  • See the account name, number, and bank information
  • Review the current balance and activity
  • Check the account status and properties
  • View recent transactions for the account
  • Access reconciliation history

Account Management Activities:

  • Updating Properties: Modify account names, numbers, or bank information
  • Activating/Deactivating: Enable or disable accounts
  • Changing GL Links: Update linked general ledger accounts
  • Adjusting Balances: Correct account balances when necessary
  • Viewing History: Review transaction history
  • Exporting Data: Download account information

Actions Available:

  • View Details: See complete account information
  • Edit Account: Modify account properties
  • Close Account: Mark account as closed
  • Export: Download account data in Excel or CSV
  • View Transactions: See all transactions for the account
  • Reconcile: Start bank reconciliation process
  • Transfer: Initiate transfers between accounts

Important: When closing an account, ensure all transactions are recorded and the account is properly reconciled before closing.

Bank Transactions

Overview

Bank transactions include all deposits, withdrawals, transfers, and other banking activities.

Transaction Types:

  • Deposits: Money added to the account
  • Withdrawals: Money taken out of the account
  • Transfers: Money moved between accounts
  • Bank Charges: Fees charged by the bank
  • Interest Earned: Interest credited to the account
  • Direct Debits: Automatic payments from the account

Transaction Recording:

  • Record transactions with proper dates
  • Link transactions to appropriate accounts
  • Include reference numbers for tracking
  • Provide descriptions for each transaction
  • Ensure transactions balance properly
  • Attach supporting documentation when needed

Transaction Management:

  • Import transactions from bank feeds
  • Manually enter transactions not captured electronically
  • Match transactions to existing records
  • Investigate and resolve discrepancies
  • Generate transaction reports
  • Export transaction data for analysis

Tip: Regular transaction recording helps maintain accurate account balances and simplifies the reconciliation process.

Bank Reconciliation

Overview

Bank reconciliation compares bank statements with internal records to ensure accuracy.

Reconciliation Process:

  1. Obtain bank statement for the period
  2. Compare ending balances
  3. Identify outstanding deposits
  4. Identify outstanding checks
  5. Account for bank charges and interest
  6. Prepare reconciliation adjustments
  7. Verify balances match after adjustments
  8. Document the reconciliation

Reconciliation Elements:

  • Bank Balance: Balance per bank statement
  • Book Balance: Balance per company records
  • Outstanding Deposits: Deposits made but not yet reflected in bank
  • Outstanding Checks: Checks written but not yet cleared
  • Bank Errors: Errors made by the bank
  • Company Errors: Errors in company records

Reconciliation Benefits:

  • Ensure accuracy of financial records
  • Identify unauthorized transactions
  • Discover timing differences
  • Validate internal controls
  • Prepare for audits
  • Maintain financial integrity

Best Practice: Perform bank reconciliations monthly to catch discrepancies early and maintain accurate records.

Permissions and Access

Overview

Access to Bank Accounts features is controlled by user roles and permissions.

Role-Based Access:

  • Super Admin: Full access to create, modify, and delete bank accounts
  • Accounting Manager: Can create and modify bank accounts
  • Accountant: Can view accounts and record transactions
  • Bookkeeper: Can view accounts and record basic transactions
  • Regular Employee: May have read-only access depending on permissions

Permission Considerations:

  • Only authorized personnel should create new bank accounts
  • Modifying account properties may require elevated permissions
  • Recording transactions typically requires appropriate access
  • Reconciliation may require specific authorization
  • Some accounts may be restricted to specific users

Tip: Contact your system administrator if you need elevated permissions for bank account management tasks.

Best Practices

Optimizing Bank Account Management

Follow these best practices to maintain effective bank account management.

Account Setup:

  • Link each bank account to the appropriate general ledger account
  • Ensure account numbers are correctly entered
  • Record accurate opening balances
  • Document bank contact information
  • Set up online banking access when available

Transaction Recording:

  • Record transactions promptly after they occur
  • Use consistent and descriptive references
  • Attach supporting documentation
  • Verify that transactions balance properly
  • Regularly import electronic transactions

Reconciliation:

  • Perform reconciliations monthly
  • Investigate all discrepancies
  • Document reconciliation adjustments
  • Retain reconciliation records
  • Review reconciliations with supervisors

Security:

  • Restrict access to authorized personnel only
  • Implement dual control for large transactions
  • Monitor account activity regularly
  • Secure online banking credentials
  • Review account statements promptly

Important: Bank account security is critical. Implement strong access controls and monitor account activity regularly.
